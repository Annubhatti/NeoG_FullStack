History

The Taj Mahal, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the Seven Wonders of the World, is an iconic symbol of India and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture. Built in Agra between 1632 and 1653 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan, the Taj Mahal serves as a mausoleum for his beloved wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Upon her death in 1631, Shah Jahan commissioned this magnificent structure in her memory, symbolizing their deep love and devotion.
