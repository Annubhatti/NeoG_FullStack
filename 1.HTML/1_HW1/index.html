Biography

Mahatma Gandhi was born into a modest family in Gujarat. His father, Karamchand Gandhi, was a local official, and his mother, Putlibai, was deeply religious. Gandhi's upbringing was influenced by the principles of Jainism, emphasizing non-violence, fasting, and vegetarianism.

Philosophy

Ahimsa (Nonviolence)

Gandhi's principles of non-violence and civil disobedience inspired millions in India and globally. He emphasized self-reliance, advocating for the use of khadi (hand-spun cloth) to promote economic independence.

Satyagraha (Truth Force)

Satyagraha is a philosophy and practice of nonviolent resistance developed by Mahatma Gandhi. Derived from Sanskrit, the term combines "Satya" (truth) and "Agraha" (firmness or insistence), meaning "holding firmly to the truth."

Legacy

Known as the Father of the Nation, Gandhi's philosophy of non-violence influenced global leaders like Martin Luther King Jr. and Nelson Mandela. Despite his assassination in 1948, his teachings on peace and justice continue to inspire.
