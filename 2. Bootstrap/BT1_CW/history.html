History

The Taj Mahal has a rich and romantic history rooted in love, loss, and imperial ambition. It was commissioned by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his favorite wife, Mumtaz Mahal, who died in 1631 during childbirth.

Despite these, it remains a globally revered monument and one of the most visited heritage sites in the world.
